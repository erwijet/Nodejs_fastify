45991892255869 What percentage of possible common causes is said to come from the system of production? Pr-Fundamental Conceptsfalse184068821016710
Explanation
The objective of this question is to test your understanding that the vast majority of problems in an operation are found in the system of production, not in the behavior of an individual that works inside such system and must follow its rules (90-95% or 94% according to Deming). On the positive side, the vast majority of the solutions can also be produced by improving the system.
However, this is ignored every time we seek a "culprit" for a problem instead of looking first for the reasons that lead to the problem. Work on the system first, where the common causes are found. 

Deming states that "most troubles and most possibilities for improvement add up to the proportions something like this: 94% belongs to the system (responsibility of management), 6% special."

Read the notion of "going beyond the person" in the introduction to the fishbone diagram in the chapter on Analyze - Qualitative analysis.
