45993112255873 “No problem is…” Pr-Fundamental Conceptsfalse184068991016730
Explanation
When a manager hears repeatedby that there are no problems in a department, this is likely a problem because it may indicate any of the following: the employees cannot identify when something is off because they lack a standard for comparison, or the employees will not bring up problems because they do not feel comfortable doing so. Both are managemen-level problems.

Expecting never having problems is illusory because all businesses and systems of production are subject to entropy which brings variation, usually in undesirable forms.

The objective of Six Sigma is to reduce variation by solving the root causes of any problems found in an operation.

"No problem is a problem" is a saying attributed to Taiichi Ohno, the inventor of the Toyota Production System.

45993112255895 What is a better way to seek quality than looking to achieve zero defects? Pr-Fundamental Conceptsfalse184069931016880
Explanation

Achieving zero defects is the old definition of quality. The new definition of world-class quality is setting the target value (required by the next part, next step, the customer or the competitive environment) and then seek to meet this target with minimum variation (Walter Shewhart, 1960).
Watch ZERO Defects 1: You don't want this at https://youtu.be/IbDwIrLvm28 and ZERO Defects 2: How to get there matters https://youtu.be/7BtwkESODtE

Every role must know what is expected of it through the writing of clear operating procedures (SOP), but just being accountable for quality is not enough without the trust to bring up quality issues and the authority to solve them.

Inspecting all products before shipping is a good practice to prevent defective products reaching the customer, but this has to be done along with building quality into each task along the process.
