45996142255871 What does Jidoka mean? Pr-Fundamental Conceptsfalse184068901016720
Explanation
In a production sense, jidoka means that when a problem occurs, the equipment stops immediately, preventing defective products from being produced.
On a large scale, jidoka means that all teams and deparments know what is their performace, have clarity of what they are supposed to accomplish (a standard), and diligently set out to close any gaps between the two.

Shutting down production may be required in some cases, but  many fixes can be applied by the employee on the spot to "save the minute."

Escalating problems excessively may be an indication of the employees' unwillingness to make decisions, which is a major managerial problem beyond the study of Lean Six Sigma.

On-the-spot troubleshooting to "save the minute, save the hour, save the shift, save the day" is an expression used by Art Smalley in his explanation of how Toyota addreses various types of problems.
